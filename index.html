If you hate flight delays

don't book flights between 11pm-3am

The average delay between US domestic flights is 9 minutes. What if I told you that depending on your decision, you can expect to wait 2 hours or depart early?

Using RITA flight delay information for US domestic flights in 2008, below you can explore the relationship between average departure delay time during different time periods.


Average flight delays by hour of day (minutes)

*Note: Red bars indicate delays of more than 40 minutes.


Clearly passangers wait longer on flights scheduled to depart from 11pm to 3am. This graph provides a quick overview of the potentially best times to fly during any day of the week. More time conscious passangers may wish to study the following grid.



Here comes the interesting part


Number of flights by hour of day

*Note: Red bars indicate number of flights less than 50,000.


Surprisingly, average flight delays appear to be inversely related to the amount of flights. In other words, hours with the highest number of flights also have the lowest average delay times. There seems to be one exception though, at 4am, there is a relatively high volume of flights and a low average weight time.


Average delay by hour of day and day of week 24/7

Like the bar chart, here we can see that the highest average delays seem to happen between 12am-3am. There seems to be no significant difference in delays between days of week. So the next question would be: do some months have longer average delays than others?

December clearly stands out as the month with longest average flight delay, over 17 minutes.
